--- Query: Dwight Yorke's impact on Manchester United during their treble-winning season ---

--- Research summary: Dwight Yorke had a transformative impact on Manchester United during their historic 1998/99 treble-winning season, serving as the catalyst that propelled the club to unprecedented success. Signed from Aston Villa at the start of that campaign, Yorke finished as the Premier League's top scorer with 18 goals, sharing the Golden Boot with Jimmy Floyd Hasselbaink and Michael Owen, while forming a legendary striking partnership with Andy Cole that became the foundation of United's attack. His contributions extended far beyond domestic competition, as he scored crucial goals against European giants including Bayern Munich, Barcelona, Inter Milan, and Juventus in the Champions League, netting 8 goals total in the competition, and added 3 more in the FA Cup to help secure all three trophies. Yorke's 18 league goals were particularly decisive, with a third of them directly earning United 11 points that proved essential to their title triumph - without these crucial strikes, the team would have finished fourth rather than champions. His impact was so significant that he was awarded the Premier League Player of the Season, and his infectious smile and positive attitude helped maintain team morale during the intense pressure of competing on three fronts simultaneously, making him an indispensable figure in what Sir Alex Ferguson later described as his greatest achievement.

**Dwight Eversley Yorke** [CM](/wiki/Chaconia_Medal "Chaconia Medal") (born 3 November 1971) is a Trinidadian and Tobagonian professional [football](/wiki/Association_football "Association football") coach and former player who is the head coach of [Trinidad and Tobago](/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago_national_football_team "Trinidad and Tobago national football team"). Throughout his club career, he played for [Aston Villa](/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C. "Aston Villa F.C."), [Manchester United](/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C. "Manchester United F.C."), [Blackburn Rovers](/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C. "Blackburn Rovers F.C."), [Birmingham City](/wiki/Birmingham_City_F.C. "Birmingham City F.C."), [Sydney FC](/wiki/Sydney_FC "Sydney FC") and [Sunderland](/wiki/Sunderland_A.F.C. "Sunderland A.F.C."), mainly as a [forward](/wiki/Forward_(association_football) "Forward (association football)"), between 1998 and 2009. Yorke formed a prolific strike partnership with [Andy Cole](/wiki/Andy_Cole "Andy Cole") at Manchester United, where he won numerous honours including several [Premier League](/wiki/Premier_League "Premier League") titles and the Treble of the Premier League, [FA Cup](/wiki/FA_Cup "FA Cup") and [UEFA Champions League](/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League "UEFA Champions League") in [1999](/wiki/1998%E2%80%9399_Manchester_United_F.C._season "1998–99 Manchester United F.C. season"). Yorke scored 123 goals in the Premier League, a record for a non-[European](/wiki/Europe "Europe") which was not broken until [Sergio Agüero](/wiki/Sergio_Ag%C3%BCero "Sergio Agüero") in 2017.[[3]](#cite_note-3)

At international level, Yorke represented [Trinidad and Tobago](/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago_national_football_team "Trinidad and Tobago national football team") on 74 occasions between 1989 and 2009, scoring 19 goals. He helped his nation reach the semi-finals of the [2000 CONCACAF Gold Cup](/wiki/2000_CONCACAF_Gold_Cup "2000 CONCACAF Gold Cup"), and later qualify for the [FIFA World Cup](/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup "FIFA World Cup") for the first time in its history, representing his country in the [2006 tournament](/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup "2006 FIFA World Cup"). After retiring from playing in 2009, Yorke became assistant manager of the Trinidad and Tobago national team, a position he held until the completion of the [qualifying matches for the 2010 FIFA World Cup](/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification "2010 FIFA World Cup qualification").

## Club career

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Dwight_Yorke&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Club career")]

### Aston Villa

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Dwight_Yorke&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Aston Villa")]

Yorke was first discovered by [Graham Taylor](/wiki/Graham_Taylor "Graham Taylor"), at the time the [Aston Villa](/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C. "Aston Villa F.C.") manager, on a tour of the West Indies in 1989. Yorke, aged 17, appeared in a team that played a [friendly](/wiki/Friendly_(association_football) "Friendly (association football)") against Aston Villa. Taylor was impressed and offered Yorke a trial at Villa. Yorke was subsequently given a permanent contract and played for the Villa Reserve and Youth sides for the majority of the 1989–90 season, making his [First Division](/wiki/Football_League_First_Division "Football League First Division") debut for Aston Villa against Crystal Palace on 24 March 1990: Crystal Palace won the game 1–0. During his time with Aston Villa, from 1989 to 1998, Yorke played initially as a right winger until the [1995–96 season](/wiki/1995%E2%80%9396_FA_Premier_League "1995–96 FA Premier League"), he then switched to [centre forward](/wiki/Forward_(association_football) "Forward (association football)") and established himself as one of the [Premier League](/wiki/Premier_League "Premier League")'s top strikers.[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*]

Yorke was an integral member of the Villa team that reached the [League Cup](/wiki/EFL_Cup "EFL Cup") Final in 1996. Villa won 3–0 against [Leeds United](/wiki/Leeds_United_F.C. "Leeds United F.C.") with Yorke getting on the scoresheet. On 30 September 1996 he scored a hat-trick against [Newcastle United](/wiki/Newcastle_United "Newcastle United") in a 4–3 defeat. Yorke appeared for Aston Villa on 284 occasions, scoring 97 times.[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*]

The circumstances of his departure from Aston Villa were controversial. John Gregory, Aston Villa's manager at that time, made it known that the club did not want to sell Yorke to Manchester United unless they were prepared to exchange striker [Andy Cole](/wiki/Andy_Cole "Andy Cole"). Yorke played for Villa on the opening day of the season at [Everton](/wiki/Everton_F.C. "Everton F.C.") on 15 August 1998, however it appeared he made little effort during the match as he was unhappy at not being allowed to leave the club. Villa were left with no option but to sell the player and he was transferred to Manchester United for £12.6 million on 20 August 1998.[[4]](#cite_note-4)

### Manchester United

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Dwight_Yorke&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Manchester United")]

In his first season Yorke was a key player in guiding his club to a unique treble of the Premier League title, [FA Cup](/wiki/FA_Cup "FA Cup") and [UEFA Champions League](/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League "UEFA Champions League"), and forming a legendary partnership with Andy Cole.[[5]](#cite_note-5) Yorke finished the season as the top league goalscorer with 18 goals and contributed goals against [Bayern Munich](/wiki/FC_Bayern_Munich "FC Bayern Munich"), [Barcelona](/wiki/FC_Barcelona "FC Barcelona"), [Inter Milan](/wiki/Inter_Milan "Inter Milan"),[[6]](#cite_note-6) and [Juventus](/wiki/Juventus_FC "Juventus FC") in the Champions League, and he won the [Premier League Player of the Season](/wiki/Premier_League_Player_of_the_Season "Premier League Player of the Season"). Yorke was also a regular member of United's [1999–2000](/wiki/1999%E2%80%932000_in_English_football "1999–2000 in English football") title winning team, contributing 23 goals in all competitions.[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*]

Despite a less successful third season, Yorke scored his [100th Premier League goal](/wiki/List_of_footballers_with_100_or_more_Premier_League_goals "List of footballers with 100 or more Premier League goals") in a 3-0 victory over [Derby County](/wiki/Derby_County_F.C. "Derby County F.C.") on 24 November 2000[[7]](#cite_note-7) and also netted a [hat-trick](/wiki/Hat-trick_(association_football) "Hat-trick (association football)") in the top of the table clash with [Arsenal](/wiki/Arsenal_F.C. "Arsenal F.C.").[[8]](#cite_note-8) In January 2002 a move to [Middlesbrough](/wiki/Middlesbrough_F.C. "Middlesbrough F.C.") fell through.[[9]](#cite_note-9) He played his last game for United that month, and was not issued a squad number for the 2002–03 season.[[10]](#cite_note-blackburn-10) Altogether he scored 65 goals for Manchester United in 152 appearances.[[11]](#cite_note-11)

### Blackburn Rovers

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Dwight_Yorke&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Blackburn Rovers")]

In July 2002, Yorke moved to [Blackburn Rovers](/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C. "Blackburn Rovers F.C.") for an initial £2 million, potentially rising to £2.6 million; United had wanted a fee of £6 million.[[10]](#cite_note-blackburn-10) He was signed to cover the long absence of [Matt Jansen](/wiki/Matt_Jansen "Matt Jansen") from a traffic accident, and was reunited with his former United strike partner Andy Cole.[[10]](#cite_note-blackburn-10)

Yorke scored 13 goals in all competitions in his first season at [Ewood Park](/wiki/Ewood_Park "Ewood Park"), including the winner on 26 October 2002 as Rovers won 2–1 at reigning champions Arsenal.[[12]](#cite_note-12) The following 4 January on his return to [Villa Park](/wiki/Villa_Park "Villa Park"), he scored twice in a 4–1 win in the FA Cup third round.[[13]](#cite_note-13) On 11 May, in the last game of the season, he opened a 4–0 win at [Tottenham Hotspur](/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur "Tottenham Hotspur") as Blackburn beat Everton to sixth place and [UEFA Cup](/wiki/UEFA_Cup "UEFA Cup") qualification.[[14]](#cite_note-14)

### Birmingham City

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Dwight_Yorke&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Birmingham City")]

On 31 August 2004, transfer deadline day, Yorke moved to [Birmingham City](/wiki/Birmingham_City_F.C. "Birmingham City F.C.") on a one-year deal with the option of a second. Instead of a loan to [Celtic](/wiki/Celtic_F.C. "Celtic F.C."), he chose to join Birmingham for a "substantial but undisclosed fee".[[15]](#cite_note-15) It was later reported as £250,000.[[16]](#cite_note-released-16)

Yorke scored on his home debut for Birmingham in a 1–1 draw against [Charlton Athletic](/wiki/Charlton_Athletic_F.C. "Charlton Athletic F.C."), with the equaliser seven minutes after replacing [Clinton Morrison](/wiki/Clinton_Morrison "Clinton Morrison").[[17]](#cite_note-17) He scored again against Graeme Souness's Newcastle on 3 October.[[18]](#cite_note-18) However, Yorke fell out of favour at [St Andrew's](/wiki/St_Andrew%27s_(stadium) "St Andrew's (stadium)") and was released by mutual consent in April 2005.[[16]](#cite_note-released-16)

### Sydney FC

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Dwight_Yorke&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: Sydney FC")]

Yorke immediately signed for [Sydney FC](/wiki/Sydney_FC "Sydney FC"),[[19]](#cite_note-19) with a salary of $1 million a season. He scored the first goal for Sydney FC in its first A-League regular season match, a diving header against the [Melbourne Victory](/wiki/Melbourne_Victory_FC "Melbourne Victory FC"). Yorke came to Sydney FC with the biggest pedigree of all players in the A-League, having won the [treble](/wiki/Treble_(association_football) "Treble (association football)") with Manchester United. Yorke scored 7 goals in the A-League, with three of them being from [penalty kicks](/wiki/Penalty_kick_(association_football) "Penalty kick (association football)"). Sydney FC coach [Pierre Littbarski](/wiki/Pierre_Littbarski "Pierre Littbarski") moved Yorke into a midfield role and gave him the team captaincy.[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*]

He played a major role in leading Sydney FC to victory in the inaugural A-League Grand Final on 5 March against regional rivals [Central Coast Mariners](/wiki/Central_Coast_Mariners_FC "Central Coast Mariners FC"). He set up the only goal, scored by [Steve Corica](/wiki/Steve_Corica "Steve Corica"), in front of a sell out crowd of over 41,000 at the [Sydney Football Stadium](/wiki/Sydney_Football_Stadium_(1988) "Sydney Football Stadium (1988)"), and was awarded the [Joe Marston Medal](/wiki/Joe_Marston_Medal "Joe Marston Medal") as best player in the grand final. Yorke was the [marquee player](/wiki/A-League#Marquee_player "A-League") for Sydney FC – meaning that his salary fell outside the salary cap. He was also a star name for the A-League as a whole. Aside from his footballing talents, the drawing power and credibility he brought both locally and internationally proved to be beneficial for the competition in its inaugural season, leading the [Football Federation Australia](/wiki/Football_Federation_Australia "Football Federation Australia") to use his image and name for the promotion of the A-League's second season.[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*]

Due to the schedule of football in Australia, Yorke returned to Manchester United for training from March to June 2006, to retain fitness for the World Cup.[[20]](#cite_note-20)

Yorke returned to Sydney FC to take part in a friendly against Everton in 2010. The game was considered his 'farewell game' as he never had the chance to say a proper farewell to the fans at Sydney FC. Everton won the match 1–0, and Yorke was substituted midway through the second half.[[21]](#cite_note-21)

### Sunderland

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Dwight_Yorke&action=edit&section=7 "Edit section: Sunderland")]

On 31 August 2006, Yorke's transfer to [Sunderland](/wiki/Sunderland_A.F.C. "Sunderland A.F.C.") was announced.[[22]](#cite_note-22) The transfer for a fee of £200,000 ([A$](/wiki/Australian_Dollar "Australian Dollar") $500,000) re-united Yorke with ex-teammate [Roy Keane](/wiki/Roy_Keane "Roy Keane"), manager of Sunderland. Yorke made his debut in the home match against Leicester City and received a rapturous standing ovation from home fans when he came on as a substitute in the first half. Yorke was used as a defensive midfielder rather than his usual striker role.[[23]](#cite_note-23) He scored his first goal for Sunderland in the 2–1 loss against Stoke.

On 2 January 2008, during Sunderland's 1–0 defeat to Blackburn, Yorke was given a red card by referee Rob Styles. Yorke stated several times he would like to return to the [A-League](/wiki/A-League "A-League"), preferably Sydney FC. However, following Sydney FC's signings at the time it seemed unlikely that Sydney would have been able to fit him under the salary cap. It was reported that Yorke play for rivals Central Coast Mariners, the team bankrolled by the man who brought him to Sydney, Peter Turnbull.[[24]](#cite_note-24)

On 11 March 2008, it was announced that the Mariners were in "advanced negotiations" with Yorke to become the marquee for two years.[[25]](#cite_note-25) However, on 1 July he signed a new one-year contract to stay with Sunderland for the 2008–09 season.[[26]](#cite_note-26) He was named [man of the match](/wiki/Man_of_the_match "Man of the match") for his performance[[27]](#cite_note-27) against Arsenal on 4 October 2008, with Sunderland drawing 1–1 at home. Following Keane's departure in December 2008, Yorke and [Neil Bailey](/wiki/Neil_Bailey "Neil Bailey") were named as assistants to [Ricky Sbragia](/wiki/Ricky_Sbragia "Ricky Sbragia").[[28]](#cite_note-28) Yorke was released at the end of the 2008–09 season.[[29]](#cite_note-29)

### Post-retirement

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Dwight_Yorke&action=edit&section=8 "Edit section: Post-retirement")]

Yorke has completed his Level B coaching badge, and in 2010 was quoted as being interested in pursuing a career in coaching, ideally with Aston Villa.[[30]](#cite_note-30) On 17 April 2011 he completed the [London Marathon](/wiki/London_Marathon "London Marathon") in a time of 3 hours and 32 minutes.[[31]](#cite_note-31) On 14 August 2011, Yorke signed a two-year deal to work for [Sky Sports](/wiki/Sky_Sports "Sky Sports") as a pundit.[[32]](#cite_note-32)

## International career

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Dwight_Yorke&action=edit&section=9 "Edit section: International career")]

Yorke was [capped](/wiki/Cap_(association_football) "Cap (association football)") 72 times for the [Trinidad and Tobago national team](/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago_national_football_team "Trinidad and Tobago national football team"), scoring 19 goals. Along with his friend [Russell Latapy](/wiki/Russell_Latapy "Russell Latapy"), Yorke was a member of the 1989 'Strike Squad', the national team which narrowly failed to qualify for the [1990 FIFA World Cup](/wiki/1990_FIFA_World_Cup "1990 FIFA World Cup").[[33]](#cite_note-33) He retired from international football in 2001 after a disagreement with coach [René Simões](/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Sim%C3%B5es "René Simões"), who cut Yorke and [Russell Latapy](/wiki/Russell_Latapy "Russell Latapy") from the squad prior to a decisive game of the [2002 World Cup qualification](/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_%E2%80%93_CONCACAF_final_round "2002 FIFA World Cup qualification – CONCACAF final round"), as both missed practice following a night partying.[[34]](#cite_note-34) However he returned to the team for the [2006 World Cup qualification](/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification "2006 FIFA World Cup qualification") campaign, in which the team qualified for the World Cup finals for the first time in their history after a 2–1 aggregate qualifying victory over [Bahrain](/wiki/Bahrain_national_football_team "Bahrain national football team").[[35]](#cite_note-35)

Yorke was captain for all of Trinidad and Tobago's games at the [2006 World Cup](/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup "2006 FIFA World Cup") and was man of the match in the 0–0 draw against [Sweden](/wiki/Sweden_men%27s_national_football_team "Sweden men's national football team"), pipping his close friend [Shaka Hislop](/wiki/Shaka_Hislop "Shaka Hislop") to the honour despite the then [West Ham](/wiki/West_Ham_United_F.C. "West Ham United F.C.") goalkeeper making several world-class saves. He was one of six players in the Trinidad squad (the others being [Brent Sancho](/wiki/Brent_Sancho "Brent Sancho"), [Dennis Lawrence](/wiki/Dennis_Lawrence "Dennis Lawrence"), [Chris Birchall](/wiki/Chris_Birchall "Chris Birchall"), [Carlos Edwards](/wiki/Carlos_Edwards "Carlos Edwards") and [Stern John](/wiki/Stern_John "Stern John")) to have played every minute of the campaign. Yorke was rated the best defensive midfielder in the opening stages of the World Cup.[[36]](#cite_note-36)

Yorke announced his retirement from international football in March 2007, choosing to focus on his club career at Sunderland.[[37]](#cite_note-37) He captained the side in Germany, and up until his retirement. However, he made a guest return appearance for a friendly against [England](/wiki/England_national_football_team "England national football team") in June 2008 after being invited by [FIFA](/wiki/FIFA "FIFA") vice-president Jack Warner.[[38]](#cite_note-38) On 10 July 2008, the [TTFF](/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago_Football_Federation "Trinidad and Tobago Football Federation") announced Yorke's return to the national team for the [2010 World Cup qualification](/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification "2010 FIFA World Cup qualification") campaign.[[39]](#cite_note-39)

On 15 October 2008, he scored his first international goal after returning from retirement against the [United States](/wiki/United_States_men%27s_national_soccer_team "United States men's national soccer team") in a 2010 World Cup qualification match. The game ended 2–1 for Trinidad and Tobago. His goal was a crucial tie-breaker scored in the 79th minute, which put Trinidad and Tobago in a great situation to advance to the next qualifying stage, needing only a tie against Cuba in their final game. On 11 February 2009, Yorke scored a 26th-minute penalty for his country, but was sent off in the dying seconds of Trinidad and Tobago's opening match of 2010 World Cup qualification against [El Salvador](/wiki/El_Salvador_national_football_team "El Salvador national football team") (2–2) after exchanging heated remarks with Mexican referee [Marco Antonio Rodríguez](/wiki/Marco_Antonio_Rodr%C3%ADguez "Marco Antonio Rodríguez") (the Trini star had voiced his disapproval of the disruptions caused by the home crowd) and was consequently banned for four games due to his use of abusive language. The suspension was later reduced to two matches.[[40]](#cite_note-40)

After being released from Sunderland and being unable to find a club before the end of the current [transfer window](/wiki/Transfer_window "Transfer window"), Yorke retired from football altogether in September 2009, and took up the post of assistant manager with the Trinidad and Tobago national team.[[41]](#cite_note-41)

## Management career

### Early career

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Dwight_Yorke&action=edit&section=11 "Edit section: Early career")]

However, Sutton appointed [Matt Gray](/wiki/Matt_Gray_(footballer,_born_1981) "Matt Gray (footballer, born 1981)") Instead.[[42]](#cite_note-42)

On 21 April 2022 it was reported that Yorke was being considered for the vacant manager's position at [Perth Glory](/wiki/Perth_Glory "Perth Glory") in the Australian [A-League Men](/wiki/A-League_Men "A-League Men").[[44]](#cite_note-44)

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Dwight_Yorke&action=edit&section=12 "Edit section: Macarthur FC")]

On 1 July 2022, Yorke became the new manager of [A-League Men](/wiki/A-League_Men "A-League Men") side [Macarthur FC](/wiki/Macarthur_FC "Macarthur FC"), replacing [Ante Milicic](/wiki/Ante_Milicic "Ante Milicic") to become the club’s second manager.[[46]](#cite_note-46) Three months later, he led the club towards its first trophy after winning the [2022 Australia Cup Final](/wiki/2022_Australia_Cup_Final "2022 Australia Cup Final").

Yorke left the Bulls in January 2023, the day after a 1–0 loss to [Adelaide United](/wiki/Adelaide_United_F.C. "Adelaide United F.C.")

## Personal life

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Dwight_Yorke&action=edit&section=14 "Edit section: Personal life")]

Yorke grew up as a [Tottenham Hotspur](/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C. "Tottenham Hotspur F.C.") fan and his first jersey was a white Tottenham Hotspur shirt.[[49]](#cite_note-49)

Yorke had a brief relationship with former model turned writer [Katie Price](/wiki/Katie_Price "Katie Price") that produced son Harvey, who was born in May 2002. He is [autistic](/wiki/Autism_spectrum "Autism spectrum") and partially blind, and has the genetic disorder [Prader–Willi syndrome](/wiki/Prader%E2%80%93Willi_syndrome "Prader–Willi syndrome").[[50]](#cite_note-50) In a 2009 interview, Yorke conceded being unfaithful to Price during their relationship, and subsequently playing no role in the upbringing of their son. He has not claimed paternity, despite a paternity test proving he is Harvey's father. He has paid no financial amount regarding Harvey's care and treatment nor visited or contacted his son.[[51]](#cite_note-51)

The [Dwight Yorke Stadium](/wiki/Dwight_Yorke_Stadium "Dwight Yorke Stadium") in [Bacolet](/wiki/Bacolet "Bacolet"), Tobago, constructed for the [2001 FIFA U-17 World Championship](/wiki/2001_FIFA_U-17_World_Championship "2001 FIFA U-17 World Championship"), was named in Yorke's honour. Yorke is a cricket fan. One of Yorke's closest friends is [Brian Lara](/wiki/Brian_Lara "Brian Lara"). During the [1999 Cricket World Cup](/wiki/1999_Cricket_World_Cup "1999 Cricket World Cup"), Yorke made regular appearances bringing on drinks for the [West Indies cricket team](/wiki/West_Indies_cricket_team "West Indies cricket team"). Yorke's older brother, [Clint Yorke](/wiki/Clint_Yorke "Clint Yorke"), is a former first-class cricketer who has represented the [Trinidad and Tobago national cricket team](/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago_national_cricket_team "Trinidad and Tobago national cricket team") as an opening batsman.[[52]](#cite_note-52)

Yorke featured extensively in an episode of Australia's *[The Biggest Loser](/wiki/The_Biggest_Loser_(Australian_TV_series) "The Biggest Loser (Australian TV series)")*, which aired on 28 February 2006. The episode involved Yorke coaching the "red team" of contestants while they competed against the "blue team" in a game of football. [Mark Rudan](/wiki/Marko_Rudan "Marko Rudan"), Yorke's Sydney FC teammate, was the manager of the "blue team".[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*]

As a member of the squad that competed at the 2006 World Cup, Yorke was awarded the [Chaconia Medal (Gold Class)](/wiki/Chaconia_Medal "Chaconia Medal"), the second highest [state decoration](/wiki/Orders,_decorations,_and_medals_of_Trinidad_and_Tobago "Orders, decorations, and medals of Trinidad and Tobago") of Trinidad and Tobago.[[53]](#cite_note-53) He was also made a Sports Ambassador for the country.[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*]

Yorke released his autobiography, *Born to Score*, in 2009.[[54]](#cite_note-54)

In December 2018, Yorke avoided bankruptcy by paying a £1.5 million debt to [HM Revenue and Customs](/wiki/HM_Revenue_and_Customs "HM Revenue and Customs").[[55]](#cite_note-55)

# Man Utd pressure 'not our problem' - Yorke

By


[Simon Stone](https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/topics/c8gr7dqlp5pt)

Chief football news reporter

* Published

**Dwight Yorke will always be remembered for his impressive goalscoring contribution to Manchester United's historic 1999 Treble-winning campaign.**

Yorke was doing the rounds with his old strike partner Andy Cole at last night's launch of '99', the impressive three-part Prime documentary recounting the events of 25 years ago.

Sir Alex Ferguson describes the Treble as his greatest achievement.

It is part of the history of the club and one of the reasons why the criticism of the current side is so fierce given they have fallen so far from such a glorious height.

Yorke is not concerned about that.

"That is not our problem," said the former striker, who was signed from Aston Villa at the start of that season. "We did what we were meant to do, what Manchester United had always been able to do, before us and after us.

"The legacy is there. It is in the history books. That is why this is one of the greatest clubs in world football.

"The job of this club is to challenge and win trophies. If you don’t do that, you are criticised. That is the harsh reality but it is the life we live."

# I fired Manchester United to the Treble - but Sir Alex Ferguson told me I was a failure

## Man United's historic 1998/99 Treble-winning campaign was made possible by the goals of Dwight Yorke - but the moment his standards dropped, Sir Alex Ferguson made him know about it.

[Comments](#comments-wrapper "Comment on this article")

[Sport](/sport/)

[**George Smith**](https://www.manchestereveningnews.co.uk/authors/george-smith/) Former Senior Football Writer

04:00, 30 Nov 2024

After winning back-to-back Premier League titles in 1996 and 1997, imagine the frustration [Manchester United](https://www.manchestereveningnews.co.uk/all-about/manchester-united-fc) must have felt at missing out on a third successive crown in 1998 by a single point.

‌

United had won four of the first five [Premier League](https://www.manchestereveningnews.co.uk/all-about/premier-league) titles, scooping two either side of being pipped to the 1995 crown by Blackburn Rovers.

‌

The Lancashire outfit, who were under the stewardship of Sir Kenny Dalglish at the time, beat United to top spot by a single point. It was the exact same scenario when Arsenal pipped United to the summit in 1998.

‌

**READ MORE:** **[I left City because I had no future - now I would be perfect for United](https://www.manchestereveningnews.co.uk/sport/football/transfer-news/left-man-city-because-no-30406894)**

**READ MORE:** **[I left United too early and regretted it - now I run a shop for a living](https://www.manchestereveningnews.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/left-manchester-united-early-regretted-29915776)**

United ended the 1997/98 season having won just the Community Shield. They exited the [FA Cup](https://www.manchestereveningnews.co.uk/all-about/fa-cup) at the fifth round stage, bowed out of the [League Cup](https://www.manchestereveningnews.co.uk/all-about/football-league-cup) at the third round stage and said farewell to the [Champions League](https://www.manchestereveningnews.co.uk/all-about/uefa-champions-league) at the quarter-final stage.

‌

It meant United, during the summer of 1998, had to come up with the plan for how they could wrestle back the Premier League title and become a stronger force in Europe. Sir [Alex Ferguson](https://www.manchestereveningnews.co.uk/all-about/sir-alex-ferguson) decided loosening the purse strings in the transfer market would help his team's case.

Jaap Stam was the first statement signing of the summer, arriving from PSV Eindhoven to strengthen the defence, before Trinidad and Tobago international Dwight Yorke arrived from Aston Villa. United signed the striker in a deal worth north of £12.5million two days before their second Premier League game of the campaign.

Yorke had established himself as one of the Premier League's most reliable goalscorers, scoring 46 times across the three proceeding Premier League campaigns prior to moving to [Old Trafford](https://www.manchestereveningnews.co.uk/all-about/old-trafford). He scored 17 goals apiece in 1995/96 and 1996/97. and netted 12 in 1997/98.

‌

United, understandably, felt Yorke was what was the missing link in their quest to try and reclaim the Premier League title. The now-53-year-old soon hit the ground running in Manchester, scoring twice on his home debut in a 4-1 win over Charlton Athletic.

Those two strikes were the first of 29 goals Yorke plundered in his maiden season at United - and 18 of them were scored in the Premier League. His goals contributed to United reclaiming the Premier League title, beating Arsenal to it by a single point.

‌

Of Yorke's 18 league goals that season, a third of them directly earned United 11 points. Without them, they would have finished fourth.

As well as scoring 18 goals en route to United reclaiming the Premier League crown, the striker, who spent four years at United, also netted eight goals to help the club win the Champions League, and plundered three to help them scoop the FA Cup.

To say United got their money's worth is an understatement. Yorke ended the season as the club's leading goalscorer, netting five more than his nearest rival, Andy Cole.

‌

Having blossomed in his first season at Old Trafford, helping United clinch a famous Treble, Ferguson challenged Yorke to build on his 1998/99 tally of 29 goals the following season. However, the former Sunderland man fell short of reaching the target Ferguson set him, resulting in him being given a response you came to expect from Ferguson, whose demands were sky-high.

"In the first season I scored 29 goals, in the second year I scored 26, and the gaffer said to me 'you're a failure'," Yorke recalled. "I was top scorer again and we won the Premier League, but he said I was a failure.

"But you look back and I get where he was coming from. This is where you need to push yourself even further by getting more than 29.

‌

"You look at [Lionel] Messi and [Cristiano] Ronaldo and that's the kind of levels he's trying to push you to, but I didn't see it then. Finishing as the top scorer with 26 goals was very rewarding for me, but the gaffer wasn't too pleased."Then in my third year I had 14 goals from 22 starts, which is not a bad return. But that wasn't good enough at United. That was the level the manager was demanding of you and I get it now."

Fresh from winning the Treble in 1998/99, United won the Premier League crown again the following season, winning it at a canter. They won 28 of their 38 matches, amassing 91 points to finish 18 clear of second-placed Arsenal.

‌

Yorke was once again their leading marksman in the league, scoring 20 goals in 32 appearances. He was, as his numbers demonstrated, often reliable in front of goal.

However, Ferguson, just as Yorke has documented, was not pleased by the fact the now-Trinidad and Tobago boss allowed his overall output to slip. Yorke, though, felt he could have scored even more goals than the 29 he did get in his first season in M16.

"I should have scored more goals," he continued. "I was disappointed I only scored 29 goals, even though that was unbelievable in my first year.

**Dwight Eversley Yorke** [CM](/wiki/Chaconia_Medal "Chaconia Medal") (born 3 November 1971) is a Trinidadian and Tobagonian professional [football](/wiki/Association_football "Association football") coach and former player who is the head coach of [Trinidad and Tobago](/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago_national_football_team "Trinidad and Tobago national football team"). Throughout his club career, he played for [Aston Villa](/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C. "Aston Villa F.C."), [Manchester United](/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C. "Manchester United F.C."), [Blackburn Rovers](/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C. "Blackburn Rovers F.C."), [Birmingham City](/wiki/Birmingham_City_F.C. "Birmingham City F.C."), [Sydney FC](/wiki/Sydney_FC "Sydney FC") and [Sunderland](/wiki/Sunderland_A.F.C. "Sunderland A.F.C."), mainly as a [forward](/wiki/Forward_(association_football) "Forward (association football)"), between 1998 and 2009. Yorke formed a prolific strike partnership with [Andy Cole](/wiki/Andy_Cole "Andy Cole") at Manchester United, where he won numerous honours including several [Premier League](/wiki/Premier_League "Premier League") titles and the Treble of the Premier League, [FA Cup](/wiki/FA_Cup "FA Cup") and [UEFA Champions League](/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League "UEFA Champions League") in [1999](/wiki/1998%E2%80%9399_Manchester_United_F.C._season "1998–99 Manchester United F.C. season"). Yorke scored 123 goals in the Premier League, a record for a non-[European](/wiki/Europe "Europe") which was not broken until [Sergio Agüero](/wiki/Sergio_Ag%C3%BCero "Sergio Agüero") in 2017.[[3]](#cite_note-3)

At international level, Yorke represented [Trinidad and Tobago](/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago_national_football_team "Trinidad and Tobago national football team") on 74 occasions between 1989 and 2009, scoring 19 goals. He helped his nation reach the semi-finals of the [2000 CONCACAF Gold Cup](/wiki/2000_CONCACAF_Gold_Cup "2000 CONCACAF Gold Cup"), and later qualify for the [FIFA World Cup](/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup "FIFA World Cup") for the first time in its history, representing his country in the [2006 tournament](/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup "2006 FIFA World Cup"). After retiring from playing in 2009, Yorke became assistant manager of the Trinidad and Tobago national team, a position he held until the completion of the [qualifying matches for the 2010 FIFA World Cup](/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification "2010 FIFA World Cup qualification").

## Club career

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Dwight_Yorke&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Club career")]

### Aston Villa

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Dwight_Yorke&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Aston Villa")]

Yorke was first discovered by [Graham Taylor](/wiki/Graham_Taylor "Graham Taylor"), at the time the [Aston Villa](/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C. "Aston Villa F.C.") manager, on a tour of the West Indies in 1989. Yorke, aged 17, appeared in a team that played a [friendly](/wiki/Friendly_(association_football) "Friendly (association football)") against Aston Villa. Taylor was impressed and offered Yorke a trial at Villa. Yorke was subsequently given a permanent contract and played for the Villa Reserve and Youth sides for the majority of the 1989–90 season, making his [First Division](/wiki/Football_League_First_Division "Football League First Division") debut for Aston Villa against Crystal Palace on 24 March 1990: Crystal Palace won the game 1–0. During his time with Aston Villa, from 1989 to 1998, Yorke played initially as a right winger until the [1995–96 season](/wiki/1995%E2%80%9396_FA_Premier_League "1995–96 FA Premier League"), he then switched to [centre forward](/wiki/Forward_(association_football) "Forward (association football)") and established himself as one of the [Premier League](/wiki/Premier_League "Premier League")'s top strikers.[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*]

Yorke was an integral member of the Villa team that reached the [League Cup](/wiki/EFL_Cup "EFL Cup") Final in 1996. Villa won 3–0 against [Leeds United](/wiki/Leeds_United_F.C. "Leeds United F.C.") with Yorke getting on the scoresheet. On 30 September 1996 he scored a hat-trick against [Newcastle United](/wiki/Newcastle_United "Newcastle United") in a 4–3 defeat. Yorke appeared for Aston Villa on 284 occasions, scoring 97 times.[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*]

The circumstances of his departure from Aston Villa were controversial. John Gregory, Aston Villa's manager at that time, made it known that the club did not want to sell Yorke to Manchester United unless they were prepared to exchange striker [Andy Cole](/wiki/Andy_Cole "Andy Cole"). Yorke played for Villa on the opening day of the season at [Everton](/wiki/Everton_F.C. "Everton F.C.") on 15 August 1998, however it appeared he made little effort during the match as he was unhappy at not being allowed to leave the club. Villa were left with no option but to sell the player and he was transferred to Manchester United for £12.6 million on 20 August 1998.[[4]](#cite_note-4)

### Manchester United

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Dwight_Yorke&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Manchester United")]

In his first season Yorke was a key player in guiding his club to a unique treble of the Premier League title, [FA Cup](/wiki/FA_Cup "FA Cup") and [UEFA Champions League](/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League "UEFA Champions League"), and forming a legendary partnership with Andy Cole.[[5]](#cite_note-5) Yorke finished the season as the top league goalscorer with 18 goals and contributed goals against [Bayern Munich](/wiki/FC_Bayern_Munich "FC Bayern Munich"), [Barcelona](/wiki/FC_Barcelona "FC Barcelona"), [Inter Milan](/wiki/Inter_Milan "Inter Milan"),[[6]](#cite_note-6) and [Juventus](/wiki/Juventus_FC "Juventus FC") in the Champions League, and he won the [Premier League Player of the Season](/wiki/Premier_League_Player_of_the_Season "Premier League Player of the Season"). Yorke was also a regular member of United's [1999–2000](/wiki/1999%E2%80%932000_in_English_football "1999–2000 in English football") title winning team, contributing 23 goals in all competitions.[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*]

Despite a less successful third season, Yorke scored his [100th Premier League goal](/wiki/List_of_footballers_with_100_or_more_Premier_League_goals "List of footballers with 100 or more Premier League goals") in a 3-0 victory over [Derby County](/wiki/Derby_County_F.C. "Derby County F.C.") on 24 November 2000[[7]](#cite_note-7) and also netted a [hat-trick](/wiki/Hat-trick_(association_football) "Hat-trick (association football)") in the top of the table clash with [Arsenal](/wiki/Arsenal_F.C. "Arsenal F.C.").[[8]](#cite_note-8) In January 2002 a move to [Middlesbrough](/wiki/Middlesbrough_F.C. "Middlesbrough F.C.") fell through.[[9]](#cite_note-9) He played his last game for United that month, and was not issued a squad number for the 2002–03 season.[[10]](#cite_note-blackburn-10) Altogether he scored 65 goals for Manchester United in 152 appearances.[[11]](#cite_note-11)

### Blackburn Rovers

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Dwight_Yorke&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Blackburn Rovers")]

In July 2002, Yorke moved to [Blackburn Rovers](/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C. "Blackburn Rovers F.C.") for an initial £2 million, potentially rising to £2.6 million; United had wanted a fee of £6 million.[[10]](#cite_note-blackburn-10) He was signed to cover the long absence of [Matt Jansen](/wiki/Matt_Jansen "Matt Jansen") from a traffic accident, and was reunited with his former United strike partner Andy Cole.[[10]](#cite_note-blackburn-10)

Yorke scored 13 goals in all competitions in his first season at [Ewood Park](/wiki/Ewood_Park "Ewood Park"), including the winner on 26 October 2002 as Rovers won 2–1 at reigning champions Arsenal.[[12]](#cite_note-12) The following 4 January on his return to [Villa Park](/wiki/Villa_Park "Villa Park"), he scored twice in a 4–1 win in the FA Cup third round.[[13]](#cite_note-13) On 11 May, in the last game of the season, he opened a 4–0 win at [Tottenham Hotspur](/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur "Tottenham Hotspur") as Blackburn beat Everton to sixth place and [UEFA Cup](/wiki/UEFA_Cup "UEFA Cup") qualification.[[14]](#cite_note-14)

### Birmingham City

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Dwight_Yorke&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Birmingham City")]

On 31 August 2004, transfer deadline day, Yorke moved to [Birmingham City](/wiki/Birmingham_City_F.C. "Birmingham City F.C.") on a one-year deal with the option of a second. Instead of a loan to [Celtic](/wiki/Celtic_F.C. "Celtic F.C."), he chose to join Birmingham for a "substantial but undisclosed fee".[[15]](#cite_note-15) It was later reported as £250,000.[[16]](#cite_note-released-16)

Yorke scored on his home debut for Birmingham in a 1–1 draw against [Charlton Athletic](/wiki/Charlton_Athletic_F.C. "Charlton Athletic F.C."), with the equaliser seven minutes after replacing [Clinton Morrison](/wiki/Clinton_Morrison "Clinton Morrison").[[17]](#cite_note-17) He scored again against Graeme Souness's Newcastle on 3 October.[[18]](#cite_note-18) However, Yorke fell out of favour at [St Andrew's](/wiki/St_Andrew%27s_(stadium) "St Andrew's (stadium)") and was released by mutual consent in April 2005.[[16]](#cite_note-released-16)

### Sydney FC

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Dwight_Yorke&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: Sydney FC")]

Yorke immediately signed for [Sydney FC](/wiki/Sydney_FC "Sydney FC"),[[19]](#cite_note-19) with a salary of $1 million a season. He scored the first goal for Sydney FC in its first A-League regular season match, a diving header against the [Melbourne Victory](/wiki/Melbourne_Victory_FC "Melbourne Victory FC"). Yorke came to Sydney FC with the biggest pedigree of all players in the A-League, having won the [treble](/wiki/Treble_(association_football) "Treble (association football)") with Manchester United. Yorke scored 7 goals in the A-League, with three of them being from [penalty kicks](/wiki/Penalty_kick_(association_football) "Penalty kick (association football)"). Sydney FC coach [Pierre Littbarski](/wiki/Pierre_Littbarski "Pierre Littbarski") moved Yorke into a midfield role and gave him the team captaincy.[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*]

He played a major role in leading Sydney FC to victory in the inaugural A-League Grand Final on 5 March against regional rivals [Central Coast Mariners](/wiki/Central_Coast_Mariners_FC "Central Coast Mariners FC"). He set up the only goal, scored by [Steve Corica](/wiki/Steve_Corica "Steve Corica"), in front of a sell out crowd of over 41,000 at the [Sydney Football Stadium](/wiki/Sydney_Football_Stadium_(1988) "Sydney Football Stadium (1988)"), and was awarded the [Joe Marston Medal](/wiki/Joe_Marston_Medal "Joe Marston Medal") as best player in the grand final. Yorke was the [marquee player](/wiki/A-League#Marquee_player "A-League") for Sydney FC – meaning that his salary fell outside the salary cap. He was also a star name for the A-League as a whole. Aside from his footballing talents, the drawing power and credibility he brought both locally and internationally proved to be beneficial for the competition in its inaugural season, leading the [Football Federation Australia](/wiki/Football_Federation_Australia "Football Federation Australia") to use his image and name for the promotion of the A-League's second season.[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*]

Due to the schedule of football in Australia, Yorke returned to Manchester United for training from March to June 2006, to retain fitness for the World Cup.[[20]](#cite_note-20)

Yorke returned to Sydney FC to take part in a friendly against Everton in 2010. The game was considered his 'farewell game' as he never had the chance to say a proper farewell to the fans at Sydney FC. Everton won the match 1–0, and Yorke was substituted midway through the second half.[[21]](#cite_note-21)

### Sunderland

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Dwight_Yorke&action=edit&section=7 "Edit section: Sunderland")]

On 31 August 2006, Yorke's transfer to [Sunderland](/wiki/Sunderland_A.F.C. "Sunderland A.F.C.") was announced.[[22]](#cite_note-22) The transfer for a fee of £200,000 ([A$](/wiki/Australian_Dollar "Australian Dollar") $500,000) re-united Yorke with ex-teammate [Roy Keane](/wiki/Roy_Keane "Roy Keane"), manager of Sunderland. Yorke made his debut in the home match against Leicester City and received a rapturous standing ovation from home fans when he came on as a substitute in the first half. Yorke was used as a defensive midfielder rather than his usual striker role.[[23]](#cite_note-23) He scored his first goal for Sunderland in the 2–1 loss against Stoke.

On 2 January 2008, during Sunderland's 1–0 defeat to Blackburn, Yorke was given a red card by referee Rob Styles. Yorke stated several times he would like to return to the [A-League](/wiki/A-League "A-League"), preferably Sydney FC. However, following Sydney FC's signings at the time it seemed unlikely that Sydney would have been able to fit him under the salary cap. It was reported that Yorke play for rivals Central Coast Mariners, the team bankrolled by the man who brought him to Sydney, Peter Turnbull.[[24]](#cite_note-24)

On 11 March 2008, it was announced that the Mariners were in "advanced negotiations" with Yorke to become the marquee for two years.[[25]](#cite_note-25) However, on 1 July he signed a new one-year contract to stay with Sunderland for the 2008–09 season.[[26]](#cite_note-26) He was named [man of the match](/wiki/Man_of_the_match "Man of the match") for his performance[[27]](#cite_note-27) against Arsenal on 4 October 2008, with Sunderland drawing 1–1 at home. Following Keane's departure in December 2008, Yorke and [Neil Bailey](/wiki/Neil_Bailey "Neil Bailey") were named as assistants to [Ricky Sbragia](/wiki/Ricky_Sbragia "Ricky Sbragia").[[28]](#cite_note-28) Yorke was released at the end of the 2008–09 season.[[29]](#cite_note-29)

### Post-retirement

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Dwight_Yorke&action=edit&section=8 "Edit section: Post-retirement")]

Yorke has completed his Level B coaching badge, and in 2010 was quoted as being interested in pursuing a career in coaching, ideally with Aston Villa.[[30]](#cite_note-30) On 17 April 2011 he completed the [London Marathon](/wiki/London_Marathon "London Marathon") in a time of 3 hours and 32 minutes.[[31]](#cite_note-31) On 14 August 2011, Yorke signed a two-year deal to work for [Sky Sports](/wiki/Sky_Sports "Sky Sports") as a pundit.[[32]](#cite_note-32)

## International career

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Dwight_Yorke&action=edit&section=9 "Edit section: International career")]

Yorke was [capped](/wiki/Cap_(association_football) "Cap (association football)") 72 times for the [Trinidad and Tobago national team](/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago_national_football_team "Trinidad and Tobago national football team"), scoring 19 goals. Along with his friend [Russell Latapy](/wiki/Russell_Latapy "Russell Latapy"), Yorke was a member of the 1989 'Strike Squad', the national team which narrowly failed to qualify for the [1990 FIFA World Cup](/wiki/1990_FIFA_World_Cup "1990 FIFA World Cup").[[33]](#cite_note-33) He retired from international football in 2001 after a disagreement with coach [René Simões](/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Sim%C3%B5es "René Simões"), who cut Yorke and [Russell Latapy](/wiki/Russell_Latapy "Russell Latapy") from the squad prior to a decisive game of the [2002 World Cup qualification](/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_%E2%80%93_CONCACAF_final_round "2002 FIFA World Cup qualification – CONCACAF final round"), as both missed practice following a night partying.[[34]](#cite_note-34) However he returned to the team for the [2006 World Cup qualification](/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification "2006 FIFA World Cup qualification") campaign, in which the team qualified for the World Cup finals for the first time in their history after a 2–1 aggregate qualifying victory over [Bahrain](/wiki/Bahrain_national_football_team "Bahrain national football team").[[35]](#cite_note-35)

Yorke was captain for all of Trinidad and Tobago's games at the [2006 World Cup](/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup "2006 FIFA World Cup") and was man of the match in the 0–0 draw against [Sweden](/wiki/Sweden_men%27s_national_football_team "Sweden men's national football team"), pipping his close friend [Shaka Hislop](/wiki/Shaka_Hislop "Shaka Hislop") to the honour despite the then [West Ham](/wiki/West_Ham_United_F.C. "West Ham United F.C.") goalkeeper making several world-class saves. He was one of six players in the Trinidad squad (the others being [Brent Sancho](/wiki/Brent_Sancho "Brent Sancho"), [Dennis Lawrence](/wiki/Dennis_Lawrence "Dennis Lawrence"), [Chris Birchall](/wiki/Chris_Birchall "Chris Birchall"), [Carlos Edwards](/wiki/Carlos_Edwards "Carlos Edwards") and [Stern John](/wiki/Stern_John "Stern John")) to have played every minute of the campaign. Yorke was rated the best defensive midfielder in the opening stages of the World Cup.[[36]](#cite_note-36)

Yorke announced his retirement from international football in March 2007, choosing to focus on his club career at Sunderland.[[37]](#cite_note-37) He captained the side in Germany, and up until his retirement. However, he made a guest return appearance for a friendly against [England](/wiki/England_national_football_team "England national football team") in June 2008 after being invited by [FIFA](/wiki/FIFA "FIFA") vice-president Jack Warner.[[38]](#cite_note-38) On 10 July 2008, the [TTFF](/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago_Football_Federation "Trinidad and Tobago Football Federation") announced Yorke's return to the national team for the [2010 World Cup qualification](/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification "2010 FIFA World Cup qualification") campaign.[[39]](#cite_note-39)

On 15 October 2008, he scored his first international goal after returning from retirement against the [United States](/wiki/United_States_men%27s_national_soccer_team "United States men's national soccer team") in a 2010 World Cup qualification match. The game ended 2–1 for Trinidad and Tobago. His goal was a crucial tie-breaker scored in the 79th minute, which put Trinidad and Tobago in a great situation to advance to the next qualifying stage, needing only a tie against Cuba in their final game. On 11 February 2009, Yorke scored a 26th-minute penalty for his country, but was sent off in the dying seconds of Trinidad and Tobago's opening match of 2010 World Cup qualification against [El Salvador](/wiki/El_Salvador_national_football_team "El Salvador national football team") (2–2) after exchanging heated remarks with Mexican referee [Marco Antonio Rodríguez](/wiki/Marco_Antonio_Rodr%C3%ADguez "Marco Antonio Rodríguez") (the Trini star had voiced his disapproval of the disruptions caused by the home crowd) and was consequently banned for four games due to his use of abusive language. The suspension was later reduced to two matches.[[40]](#cite_note-40)

After being released from Sunderland and being unable to find a club before the end of the current [transfer window](/wiki/Transfer_window "Transfer window"), Yorke retired from football altogether in September 2009, and took up the post of assistant manager with the Trinidad and Tobago national team.[[41]](#cite_note-41)

## Management career

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Dwight_Yorke&action=edit&section=10 "Edit section: Management career")]

### Early career

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Dwight_Yorke&action=edit&section=11 "Edit section: Early career")]

On 21 April 2019, Yorke was interviewed for the vacant role at [National League](/wiki/National_League_(division) "National League (division)") side [Sutton United](/wiki/Sutton_United "Sutton United"). However, Sutton appointed [Matt Gray](/wiki/Matt_Gray_(footballer,_born_1981) "Matt Gray (footballer, born 1981)") Instead.[[42]](#cite_note-42)

On 12 November 2021, [Aston Villa](/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C. "Aston Villa F.C.") appointed [Steven Gerrard](/wiki/Steven_Gerrard "Steven Gerrard") as their new manager. Yorke said he was disappointed not to be hired instead.[[43]](#cite_note-43)

On 21 April 2022 it was reported that Yorke was being considered for the vacant manager's position at [Perth Glory](/wiki/Perth_Glory "Perth Glory") in the Australian [A-League Men](/wiki/A-League_Men "A-League Men").[[44]](#cite_note-44)

On 25 May 2022, Yorke managed the [A-Leagues All Stars](/wiki/A-Leagues_All_Stars_Game "A-Leagues All Stars Game") in their 3–2 loss against [FC Barcelona](/wiki/FC_Barcelona "FC Barcelona").[[45]](#cite_note-45)

### Macarthur FC

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Dwight_Yorke&action=edit&section=12 "Edit section: Macarthur FC")]

On 1 July 2022, Yorke became the new manager of [A-League Men](/wiki/A-League_Men "A-League Men") side [Macarthur FC](/wiki/Macarthur_FC "Macarthur FC"), replacing [Ante Milicic](/wiki/Ante_Milicic "Ante Milicic") to become the club’s second manager.[[46]](#cite_note-46) Three months later, he led the club towards its first trophy after winning the [2022 Australia Cup Final](/wiki/2022_Australia_Cup_Final "2022 Australia Cup Final").

Yorke left the Bulls in January 2023, the day after a 1–0 loss to [Adelaide United](/wiki/Adelaide_United_F.C. "Adelaide United F.C.")
[[47]](#cite_note-47)

### Trinidad and Tobago

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Dwight_Yorke&action=edit&section=13 "Edit section: Trinidad and Tobago")]

On 1 November 2024, Yorke became the new manager of the [Trinidad and Tobago national team](/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago_national_football_team "Trinidad and Tobago national football team"), succeeding [Angus Eve](/wiki/Angus_Eve "Angus Eve").[[48]](#cite_note-48)

## Personal life

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Dwight_Yorke&action=edit&section=14 "Edit section: Personal life")]

Yorke grew up as a [Tottenham Hotspur](/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C. "Tottenham Hotspur F.C.") fan and his first jersey was a white Tottenham Hotspur shirt.[[49]](#cite_note-49)

Yorke had a brief relationship with former model turned writer [Katie Price](/wiki/Katie_Price "Katie Price") that produced son Harvey, who was born in May 2002. He is [autistic](/wiki/Autism_spectrum "Autism spectrum") and partially blind, and has the genetic disorder [Prader–Willi syndrome](/wiki/Prader%E2%80%93Willi_syndrome "Prader–Willi syndrome").[[50]](#cite_note-50) In a 2009 interview, Yorke conceded being unfaithful to Price during their relationship, and subsequently playing no role in the upbringing of their son. He has not claimed paternity, despite a paternity test proving he is Harvey's father. He has paid no financial amount regarding Harvey's care and treatment nor visited or contacted his son.[[51]](#cite_note-51)

The [Dwight Yorke Stadium](/wiki/Dwight_Yorke_Stadium "Dwight Yorke Stadium") in [Bacolet](/wiki/Bacolet "Bacolet"), Tobago, constructed for the [2001 FIFA U-17 World Championship](/wiki/2001_FIFA_U-17_World_Championship "2001 FIFA U-17 World Championship"), was named in Yorke's honour. Yorke is a cricket fan. One of Yorke's closest friends is [Brian Lara](/wiki/Brian_Lara "Brian Lara"). During the [1999 Cricket World Cup](/wiki/1999_Cricket_World_Cup "1999 Cricket World Cup"), Yorke made regular appearances bringing on drinks for the [West Indies cricket team](/wiki/West_Indies_cricket_team "West Indies cricket team"). Yorke's older brother, [Clint Yorke](/wiki/Clint_Yorke "Clint Yorke"), is a former first-class cricketer who has represented the [Trinidad and Tobago national cricket team](/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago_national_cricket_team "Trinidad and Tobago national cricket team") as an opening batsman.[[52]](#cite_note-52)

Yorke featured extensively in an episode of Australia's *[The Biggest Loser](/wiki/The_Biggest_Loser_(Australian_TV_series) "The Biggest Loser (Australian TV series)")*, which aired on 28 February 2006. The episode involved Yorke coaching the "red team" of contestants while they competed against the "blue team" in a game of football. [Mark Rudan](/wiki/Marko_Rudan "Marko Rudan"), Yorke's Sydney FC teammate, was the manager of the "blue team".[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*]

As a member of the squad that competed at the 2006 World Cup, Yorke was awarded the [Chaconia Medal (Gold Class)](/wiki/Chaconia_Medal "Chaconia Medal"), the second highest [state decoration](/wiki/Orders,_decorations,_and_medals_of_Trinidad_and_Tobago "Orders, decorations, and medals of Trinidad and Tobago") of Trinidad and Tobago.[[53]](#cite_note-53) He was also made a Sports Ambassador for the country.[*[citation needed](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*]

Yorke released his autobiography, *Born to Score*, in 2009.[[54]](#cite_note-54)

In December 2018, Yorke avoided bankruptcy by paying a £1.5 million debt to [HM Revenue and Customs](/wiki/HM_Revenue_and_Customs "HM Revenue and Customs").[[55]](#cite_note-55)
